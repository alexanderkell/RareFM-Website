About



Rare FM

 Rare FM is UCL Union's official student radio.
Founded in 1998, it has since been playing the best in student's music taste across campus, online and on FM.
Students host radio shows every day of the week, 17 hours a day, from 9am til 2am. Any UCL student can apply to host a show, pending an application and membership of the station.

Music Events

 Rare FM hosts nights throughout the year, including DJ sets at the Freshers' Ball at Ministry of Sound, a major opening party, a Rare Sessions gig night and other events hosted in various venues throughout London. In addition to this, Rare FM organises weekly socials for members to taste music from a range of genres from different clubs throughout London.

Extra-University Relationships

 Rare FM maintains relationships out of the university, notably with the charity IntoUniversity. IntoUniversity provides holiday workshops for kids from underprivileged backgrounds, in the hope that this will give them higher aspirations. Rare FM provides radio show workshops for the children, and invites them to come into Rare FM's studio to host their own shows. All of which are uploaded as podcasts and can be listened to on the station's Mixcloud.

Under City Lights

 Rare FM has its own music magazine, Under City Lights. Under City lights has both an online and printed platform, with the printed edition handed out around campus at the beginning of each term. Under City Lights conducts many interviews with bands around London, interviews with artists such as The Darkness, Marie Naffah (MTV Unsigned Act Winner) and Alexander Teller. Under City Lights is always open to new student writers, the editors of the magazine are to be contacted to register interest.
